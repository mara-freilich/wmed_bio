Mathematical modeling approach to phytoplankton biogeography¶

$\frac{dP}{dt} = \underbrace{G_P}_\text{maximum growth rate} \underbrace{(1-e^{-\gamma I(z)/I_0})}_\text{limitation by light}\underbrace{\frac{N}{k_P+N}}_\text{limitation by nutrients}P-\underbrace{m_1 P - m_2 P^2}_\text{mortality}$

Monod function¶

$\frac{N}{k+N}$

  • Derived from chemical kinetics
  • Parameter is a half saturation constant
    • This parameter determines growth rate at low nutrient concentration
    • Small cells have lower half saturation constant due to surface area to volume ratio

If N = k

$\frac{N}{k+N} = \frac{k}{2k} = \frac{1}{2}$

Maximum growth rate¶

  • Maximum growth rate depends on cell size
  • as phytoplankton species become larger, their ability to take up nitrogen, when available in large concentrations, increases

Light limitation¶

The typical euphotic depth (or depth where there is enough light to grow) is 100 meters, but it varies globally. The euphotic depth is usually defined as 1% of the surface light level.

Mortality¶

There are two terms used for mortality.

  • The linear term $m_1P$ represents respiration.
  • The non-linear term $m_1P^2$ represents density-dependent loss, for example due to predation
